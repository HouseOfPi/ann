The Science of Robot Learning

1 Experience

Instead of manual programming, the robot learns through Experience. At first, it doesn't know what actions are good or bad. It simply tries moving, passing, or shooting and observes the outcome.

2 Feedback

Every move result in a Reinforcement Signal. Good outcomes (scoring) trigger positive rewards, while poor results (missing) assign negative penalties. This is how the robot learns "value".

3 Rewiring

The robot uses Backpropagation to update its internal neural connections. It strengthens the weights that led to high rewards and weakens those that caused errors, effectively "rewiring" its brain.

4 Strategy

Through billions of weight updates, the robot moves from random movement to Complex Strategy. It stops looking at individual kicks and starts recognizing patterns that maximize its success.
